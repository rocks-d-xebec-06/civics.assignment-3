The Nikahnama Gap

Analyzing Legal Literacy & Marital Rights Among University Women in Pakistan

Research Context

The "Nikahnama Gap" identifies the discrepancy between a woman's academic credentials and her practical knowledge of the marriage contract. Often, Clause 18 (Right to Divorce) is crossed out by third parties, stripping women of legal agency before the marriage begins.

The 4 Pillars of Protection

Clause 13: Mehr

Ensuring dower is "Muajjal" (immediate) recognizes a woman's economic rights and provides financial security from the start.

Clause 18: Divorce

Talaq-e-Tafweez (delegated right) allows a woman to dissolve a marriage without losing her dower or facing court delays.

Clause 20: Conditions

A crucial space to document specific rights such as continued education, career pursuit, or polygamy restrictions.

Clause 22: Maintenance

Clearly defines the husband's legal obligation to provide financial support consistent with the couple's lifestyle.

The IPV Connection

Intimate Partner Violence (IPV) is often sustained through legal entrapment. When legal clauses are removed, a woman's exit route from abuse becomes difficult. Legal literacy is the first line of defense in reducing IPV vulnerability by ensuring women enter marriage with full legal agency.
